In an ideal situation the events of a co-op work term should guide the future studies and career of the student participating in it by helping them learn about themselves and how they can be successful. A co-op work term generally serves as the first position where the student has the chance to take what they have learned in class, and apply that knowledge to a workplace setting. A co-op work term gives the student the chance to see how what they have been learning is relevant and applicable to the industry. It also serves as a bit of a testing ground - the student gets the opportunity to test a certain career path within their respective field.

Heading into my first work term I had some goals and aspirations, for both how the work term itself would go and what I would learn walking away from it, and take into my future. I wanted to learn new programming languages and software practices, but I also wanted to learn about myself; I wanted to learn what a career in software development might be like for me, and, if it is something that I decide I would like to pursue, how I can be most successful in one.

This website describes what I aimed to learn from my work term, what it was like, and what I took away from it and will carry into my future.
